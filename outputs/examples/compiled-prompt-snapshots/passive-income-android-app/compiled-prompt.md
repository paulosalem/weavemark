# Passive-income Android app specification

Income Horizon is a native Android app for people pursuing financial independence through passive income and disciplined capital growth.

The app helps the user know, before spending, how much passive income is likely to be available this month and in future months. Its primary answer is: "How much can I consume this month without compromising my capital-growth plan?" It must make the tradeoff between consumption, reinvestment, reserves, taxes, and capital growth visible without becoming a trading terminal or a generic budgeting app.

## Product surface

- Primary interaction happens on Android phones, with tablet layouts only when they improve clarity.
- The app must feel useful in short sessions: open the app, understand the current safe-to-spend position, adjust one assumption, and leave with a clear next action.
- The home screen must put the most important current answer above the fold: this month’s safe-to-spend amount, confidence, the key deductions or buffers, and whether the capital-growth plan remains healthy.
- Use Android platform conventions for navigation, back behavior, permissions, system share sheets, notifications, biometrics, accessibility, dark theme, and locale-aware formatting.
- Prefer local-first behavior for sensitive personal financial data. Core planning, historical review, and read access must work offline.
- Avoid unnecessary account creation. Require sign-in only for optional sync, backup, sharing, or paid cloud features.
- Optional sync or backup may exist, but every external connection must be explained before it is enabled.

## Financial planning model

Income Horizon plans passive income and capital growth as connected concepts.

Core concepts:
- Passive income source: any recurring or expected income stream produced by invested capital, rental assets, royalties, private credit, dividends, coupons, distributions, or similar sources.
- Capital base: the invested assets that should continue compounding unless the user explicitly models principal drawdown.
- Safe-to-spend amount: income that remains available after taxes, reserves, reinvestment, and capital-protection buffers.
- Growth target: the desired real or nominal growth rate for the capital base after consumption.
- Confidence level: how reliable a forecasted income amount is, based on source history, contractual certainty, volatility, concentration, and market sensitivity.

The app must preserve these distinctions:
- Income generated by capital is different from selling capital.
- Confirmed income is different from projected income.
- Recurring income is different from one-time inflows.
- Unused monthly income may be reinvested, reserved, carried forward, or consumed.
- A plan that depends on reducing principal is a principal-drawdown plan unless the drawdown is explicitly modeled.
- Passive-income projections are planning aids, not financial advice, guarantees, or promises of returns.
- Negative outcomes must be named plainly as shortfall, capital erosion risk, stale assumption, income delayed, unsafe consumption, or insufficient reinvestment.

## Forecasting requirements

The app must forecast monthly passive income and safe-to-spend amounts for at least the next 12 months.

Each forecast starts from explicit sources, schedules, assumptions, tax estimates, reinvestment rules, and safety buffers. A forecasted number is incomplete without its assumptions.

The calculation model must include:
- Expected passive income by month.
- Confirmed versus projected income.
- Taxes or withholding estimates.
- Required reinvestment to preserve the chosen capital-growth target.
- Liquidity reserve or buffer before money is considered safe to spend.
- Capital-protection buffer.
- Variance between planned and actual income.
- Capital erosion risk when consumption, taxes, missed income, or delayed income would force principal drawdown.

Safe-to-spend should be calculated as passive income received or expected, minus taxes and required reserves, minus planned reinvestment, minus capital-protection buffer.

The app must compare conservative, expected, and optimistic scenarios so users do not plan around best-case income. Scenario differences must be visible near the affected numbers and should explain which assumptions changed.

The app must make capital erosion visible when consumption, taxes, or missed income would force principal drawdown. If drawdown is allowed, the forecast must label it explicitly rather than presenting it as ordinary passive-income spending.

Time, currency, locale, and rounding choices affect the meaning of the result and must be handled explicitly in domain logic rather than hidden in UI formatting.

## Material information and data boundaries

Do not include detailed database table definitions unless a field is critical to forecasting correctness, privacy, or user trust.

The product must capture the following material information:
- An income source has a name, currency, expected amount, payment cadence, next expected payment date, confidence level, market sensitivity, and last confirmation date.
- A capital pool has current value, currency, growth target, reinvestment rule, and whether principal drawdown is allowed.
- A monthly plan has expected income, confirmed income, tax estimate, reserve target, planned reinvestment, safe-to-spend amount, and actual consumption.
- A forecast has an assumption set, scenario label, generated timestamp, and warning flags.

The natural vocabulary of the app includes financial independence, passive income, capital base, capital growth, safe-to-spend, reinvestment, reserve, forecast, confidence, and capital erosion. Avoid guarantee language: passive income is not guaranteed income, expected returns are not guaranteed returns, and wealth growth is not automatic.

## Required views

The app must provide:

- Today card: the main safe-to-spend answer for the current month, confidence, the most important deductions or buffers, and the current health/status signal.
- Monthly calendar or timeline: expected events, confirmed events, forecast confidence, and shortfalls or surpluses for the current month, next month, and a 12-month lookahead.
- Scenario panel: conservative, expected, and optimistic scenario comparison with visible assumption differences.
- Health card: whether the user’s plan preserves the capital-growth target and whether consumption would create capital erosion risk.
- Alerts: material threshold changes only, such as a projected shortfall, missed payment, unsafe consumption, stale assumption, delayed income, or insufficient reinvestment.
- Review trail: edited assumptions, actual income entries, confirmations, and major forecast changes.

Prefer plain-language status cards over dense tables. Show explanations before advanced controls. Let users change assumptions inline and immediately see the effect. Make editing lightweight and reversible.

Exports should remain simple: monthly summary, assumptions, scenario comparison, and review trail.

## Privacy and trust

- Store sensitive financial data encrypted at rest on device.
- Let users export and delete their data.
- Explain every external connection before it is enabled.
- Keep bank, brokerage, cloud, or sync integrations optional unless the user explicitly enables them.
- Treat calculations as planning aids based on visible assumptions.
- Do not imply guaranteed financial outcomes.

## Android implementation stack

Build the app as a modern native Android application.

- Language: Kotlin.
- UI: Jetpack Compose with Material 3 components and adaptive layouts.
- State: ViewModel plus Kotlin coroutines and Flow or StateFlow.
- Persistence: Room for structured local data and DataStore for preferences.
- Background work: WorkManager for scheduled refreshes, reminders, imports, and recurring calculations.
- Dependency injection: Hilt or another explicit dependency-injection mechanism.
- Navigation: Jetpack Navigation for Compose.

Use a layered architecture with UI, presentation/state, domain, and data layers. Keep financial calculations in deterministic domain services that can be tested without Android UI dependencies. Keep platform integrations behind small interfaces so forecasting, import, notification, backup, and optional sync behavior can be tested directly.

Persist UI state across rotation, process death, and app restarts.

## Testing and quality

- Unit-test financial calculations, safe-to-spend logic, capital erosion detection, date handling, currency handling, rounding, scenario comparisons, and stale-assumption warnings.
- Add Compose UI tests for the primary dashboard, assumption editing, scenario comparison, empty states, and warning states.
- Use fake clocks and fake repositories in tests for deterministic projections.
- Include migration tests for persisted local data.
- Treat accessibility checks as part of the release checklist.
- Provide accessible color contrast, large tap targets, screen-reader labels, and reduced-motion support.

## Acceptance criteria

- A user can open Income Horizon and immediately see this month’s safe-to-spend amount, confidence, and whether the capital-growth plan is intact.
- A user can inspect the current month, next month, and at least 12 future months.
- A user can distinguish confirmed income from projected income.
- A user can compare conservative, expected, and optimistic scenarios.
- A user can see how taxes, reserves, reinvestment, and capital-protection buffers reduce spendable income.
- A user is warned when consumption, taxes, missed income, or delayed income would force principal drawdown.
- The app remains useful offline and stores sensitive financial data encrypted at rest.
- The core calculation services are deterministic, testable, and separate from Android UI code.
