# Investment brief

Write an educational investment brief for NVDA for a portfolio committee with a 12 months time horizon.

Use a clear, sober, evidence-oriented style. Avoid hype, unsupported certainty, and investment advice phrased as instructions to buy, sell, or hold. Treat the brief as educational analysis and decision support, not financial, legal, tax, accounting, brokerage, or personalized investment advice. Include the phrase "not financial advice" in the final brief.

Use the following source material as the starting point:

Revenue growth remains strong, data-center demand is high, gross margins are elevated, customer concentration and export controls are material risks, and valuation already prices in continued execution.

When available, use verified recent financial metrics before relying on memory. If data is missing, stale, ambiguous, or conflicting, say so and do not invent values. Separate supplied inputs, retrieved data, deterministic calculations, assumptions, interpretation, and decision-support options.

Keep the memo concise. Focus on decision relevance, downside, evidence quality, timing, and the smallest useful next research step.

## Analytical standards

- State facts, assumptions, and interpretations separately.
- Surface downside risk before action-oriented possibilities.
- Do not guarantee returns, prices, yields, forecasts, tax outcomes, or risk reductions.
- Frame actions as options or research triggers, not instructions to buy, sell, or hold.
- Identify the strongest counter-argument to each major claim.
- For estimates or judgments, state confidence as low, medium, or high and explain the basis.
- Prefer concrete evidence, numbers, dates, source names, and method notes over broad assertions.
- If the evidence is not strong enough for a firm conclusion, say so directly.

## Evidence quality requirements

For every important claim, judge the evidence using these dimensions:

| Criterion | What to check |
| --- | --- |
| Relevance | Does the evidence directly support or challenge the claim, or is it only adjacent? |
| Specificity | Is it based on concrete facts, numbers, examples, or observations rather than vague commentary? |
| Freshness | Is it current enough for a 12 months investment horizon? |
| Independence | Does it come from multiple independent sources or methods, or from the same source family repeated? |
| Contradictions | Are tensions and contrary evidence surfaced rather than ignored? |

Include an evidence grade: strong, adequate, weak, or insufficient. Do not upgrade the grade merely because a conclusion is plausible. Also state the main evidence gap and the decision impact: whether the available evidence is enough to act, wait, or investigate further.

## Explainability requirements

Make the reasoning traceable. Start each section with its key finding, then show the reasoning and caveats. Include a compact reasoning chain where useful:

| Step | Claim or inference | Evidence or basis | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | claim | source, calculation, assumption, or observation | low/medium/high |

Also include key assumptions, checks performed, limits, and a simplest plain-language explanation of the assessment.

## Comparative alternatives requirements

Compare NVDA against practical alternatives rather than evaluating it in isolation. Within the Alternatives section, include:

- Leading option: option
- Runner-up: option
- Decisive criterion: criterion
- Confidence: low | medium | high

Then provide a compact comparison table:

| Criterion | NVDA | Alternative 1 | Alternative 2 | Winner | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| criterion | assessment | assessment | assessment | option | decision relevance |

End the Alternatives section with:

- Best if: when each option is the right choice.
- Avoid if: when each option is the wrong choice.
- Ranking trigger: what evidence or constraint would change the order.

## Watchlist requirement

Add a watchlist table with:

| Metric | Current reading | Threshold that would change the assessment | Evidence needed next |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Required output format

Return exactly these sections and no extra top-level sections:

1. Thesis
2. Evidence
3. Risks
4. Alternatives
5. Open questions
6. Decision trigger

Within those sections, satisfy the evidence-quality, finance-safety, explainability, comparative-alternatives, and watchlist requirements above.
