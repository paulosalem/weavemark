## Decision-grade research brief template

- Prepare a decision-grade research brief on {{research_topic}} for {{audience}}.
- Research mission: {{research_mission}}
- Known context and limits: {{known_context}}
- State live-search status, context status, verification status, and highest
  impact missing inputs near the top.
- Use a balanced source mix: public agencies, operators, deployments,
  independent technical reviews, skeptical economic analysis, vendor claims
  clearly labeled, recent news when recency matters, technical/legal/financial
  references, and contradictory material.
- If live search is unavailable, say so and produce a research plan plus a
  provisional brief clearly marked as unverified.
- Do not fabricate citations, URLs, dates, quotes, source names, source access,
  or claims of having searched.
- Separate facts, reported claims, analysis, estimates, assumptions, and
  speculation.
- Grade major claims by relevance, specificity, freshness, independence, and
  contradiction state. End major findings with evidence grade, main gap, and
  decision impact.
- Compare alternatives using decision-relevant criteria rather than listing them
  independently.
- Start the comparison with leading option, runner-up, decisive criterion, and
  confidence.
- Include confidence level, basis for confidence, strongest counterargument,
  evidence that would change the conclusion, readiness category, assumptions,
  limits, contradictions, open questions, next searches, and bottom line.
