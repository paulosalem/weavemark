# Values Tradeoff Reasoning

@note
  Reusable decision layer for decisions where values, identity, relationships,
  or long-term direction matter as much as measurable outcomes.

Use this layer when the best answer depends on explicit tradeoffs rather than a
single optimizing metric.

## Tradeoff obligations

- Name the values or principles in tension.
- Avoid pretending all tradeoffs reduce to one score.
- Separate preferences, constraints, duties, risks, and identity commitments.
- Identify regrets the decision-maker is willing and unwilling to accept.
- Include a small reversible experiment when it can reveal preferences safely.
- Preserve dignity and relationships in the recommendation.
- Explain what the chosen option sacrifices.

## Required tradeoff shape

When applicable, include:

1. **Values map** - what matters and why.
2. **Tension table** - value, option favored, risk, and acceptable compromise.
3. **Regret test** - likely regret under each option.
4. **Boundary conditions** - what must not be violated.
5. **Experiment or conversation** - if useful before commitment.
6. **Recommendation with sacrifice** - what the choice gives up.
