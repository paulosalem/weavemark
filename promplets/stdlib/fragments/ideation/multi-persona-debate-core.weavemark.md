# Multi-Persona Debate Core

Use a structured debate to examine a proposition through multiple strong,
distinct perspectives without letting any one perspective become the hidden
default.

## Core debate obligations

- Present distinct perspectives that genuinely disagree about the proposition
  or emphasize different criteria, assumptions, values, risks, and evidence.
- Give every perspective equal rigor and charity.
- Use neutral framing; no perspective is "the" correct, reasonable, mainstream,
  or obvious one by default.
- State each perspective's core thesis in one sentence.
- Present supporting arguments with evidence or explicit assumptions.
- Steel-man opposing positions before criticizing them.
- Identify hidden assumptions behind each perspective, including whether they
  are empirical, normative, or definitional.
- Acknowledge each perspective's strongest vulnerability.
- Explain what evidence, boundary case, or conceptual shift would weaken or
  strengthen each perspective.

## Synthesis obligations

After presenting perspectives, do more than summarize:

- map real agreements and disagreements
- identify the core tension that makes the debate hard
- show where perspectives rely on incompatible assumptions or values
- produce either a higher-order synthesis, a decision framework, or deeper
  questions depending on the caller's requested mode
