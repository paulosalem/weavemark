# Strategic Problem Analysis

Analyze an ambiguous, consequential problem as a strategic decision rather than
as a generic advice request.

## Strategic framing obligations

- State the decision to be made in action-changing terms.
- Separate the current situation, the options under consideration, and the
  decision criteria.
- Identify constraints that cannot be wished away: time, money, attention,
  health, legal commitments, institutional rules, family obligations, or
  opportunity costs.
- Distinguish facts, assumptions, unknowns, and judgment calls.
- Explain what would make each option attractive, risky, reversible, or
  dominated.
- Include the strongest counter-argument to the recommended path.
- Produce a recommendation that can be acted on, delayed, falsified, or revised
  as new evidence arrives.

## Required strategic output

The analysis must include:

1. The decision and why it matters now.
2. The realistic options.
3. The criteria that should decide among those options.
4. The decisive uncertainties.
5. The evidence that would most improve the decision.
6. A recommendation with confidence level, caveats, and next actions.
