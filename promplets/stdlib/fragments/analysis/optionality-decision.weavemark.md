# Optionality Decision Lens

Use this lens when the decision is not only "which option has the highest
expected value?" but also "which option preserves or creates valuable future
choices?"

## Optionality obligations

- Evaluate reversibility: how difficult, costly, or time-sensitive it would be
  to undo each option.
- Evaluate option value: which choices keep more attractive future paths open.
- Evaluate downside protection: what happens if assumptions are wrong, income
  falls, costs rise, health worsens, or markets underperform.
- Evaluate timing: which choices benefit from waiting, experimenting, or
  sequencing, and which decay if delayed.
- Prefer staged commitments when uncertainty is high and the cost of learning is
  lower than the cost of a premature irreversible move.
- Define triggers that convert "wait and learn" into "act now" or "do not act."

## Required optionality output

For each major option, state:

- what future choices it opens
- what future choices it closes
- whether it is reversible, partially reversible, or hard to reverse
- what evidence would justify exercising the option
- what threshold would stop the option from being prudent
