# Research Rigor Guideline

@note
  Reusable guideline for research work that needs breadth, source discipline,
  skepticism, and transparent limits.

Use this guideline when an answer should synthesize outside information,
compare claims, or prepare a research brief.

## Source-family coverage

Prefer a balanced source mix when sources are available:

- primary or official material;
- recent news or event coverage when recency matters;
- expert, practitioner, or independent analysis;
- technical, legal, financial, scientific, or reference material where relevant;
- skeptical, contradictory, or competing-source material.

## Research obligations

- State whether live web/search access is available. If it is not available,
  separate what can be answered from general knowledge from what requires
  verification.
- Do not fabricate citations, URLs, publication dates, quotes, or source names.
- Distinguish facts, reported claims, analysis, estimates, and speculation.
- Prefer specific evidence over broad commentary.
- Surface contradictory evidence instead of smoothing it away.
- Explain source freshness requirements and whether the available evidence meets
  them.
- Include the highest-value next searches or documents if the evidence is thin.

## Required research output

When useful, include:

| Field | Content |
| --- | --- |
| Research status | verified / partially verified / unverified / research plan only |
| Source mix | source families used or needed |
| Strongest evidence | facts or sources that most support the conclusion |
| Contradictions | evidence that challenges or complicates the conclusion |
| Confidence | low / medium / high and why |
| Next searches | targeted queries or source types that would improve confidence |
