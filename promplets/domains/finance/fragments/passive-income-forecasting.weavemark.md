# Financial Method: Passive-Income Forecasting

Passive-income forecasting is the practice of estimating spendable monthly
income from passive-income sources while preserving a capital-growth plan.

### Core Forecasting Model

A passive-income forecast starts from explicit sources, schedules, assumptions,
tax estimates, reinvestment rules, and safety buffers. Its central question is
how much can be consumed without accidentally turning capital growth into capital
erosion.

### Calculation Components

- Expected passive income by month for at least the next 12 months.
- Confirmed versus projected income.
- Taxes or withholding estimates.
- Required reinvestment to preserve the chosen capital-growth target.
- Liquidity reserve or buffer before money is considered safe to spend.
- Safe-to-spend amount:
  - passive income received or expected
  - minus taxes and required reserves
  - minus planned reinvestment
  - minus capital-protection buffer
- Conservative, expected, and optimistic scenarios.
- Variance between planned and actual income.
- Capital erosion risk when consumption, taxes, missed income, or delayed income
  would force principal drawdown.

### Forecast Semantics

- A forecasted number is incomplete without its assumptions.
- Recurring passive income differs from one-time inflows.
- Income generated by capital differs from selling capital.
- Unused monthly income has four common treatments: reinvested, reserved, carried
  forward, or consumed.
- Consumption that depends on reducing principal is principal drawdown unless the
  drawdown is explicitly modeled.
- Projections are planning aids, not financial advice or guarantees.
- Time, currency, locale, and rounding choices affect the meaning of the result.

### Evidence and Review

- Forecast reliability depends on when each income source was last confirmed.
- Stale assumptions weaken the forecast.
- The change since the previous forecast is part of the forecast's meaning.
- Edited assumptions, actual income entries, and major forecast changes form the
  review trail.
