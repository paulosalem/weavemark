# Financial Resilience Lens

Use this lens for household financial decisions where resilience matters more
than a single-point wealth projection.

## Financial-resilience obligations

- Separate durable facts from assumptions and planning heuristics.
- Evaluate liquidity, spending flexibility, income dependence, debt load,
  insurance exposure, tax sensitivity, and investment volatility.
- Quantify runway and thresholds using the household's numbers.
- Avoid generic financial advice. Tie claims to explicit assumptions and
  concrete action thresholds.
- Treat projections as planning aids, not guarantees.
- Identify the assumptions most likely to change the recommendation.

## Required calculations when inputs are available

- Invested-assets coverage of current annual spending.
- Invested-assets coverage of target work-optional annual spending.
- Emergency-fund coverage in months at current spending.
- Emergency-fund coverage in months at target spending.
- Implied annual portfolio support at the stated long-run real return.
- Gap between target spending and portfolio-supported spending.
- Approximate capital required for the target spending level under at least two
  withdrawal-rate or return assumptions, including one conservative case.
- Dependence on current income to sustain the present investment pace.

## Required decision thresholds

State concrete thresholds for:

- minimum emergency-fund coverage
- minimum reliable income floor
- maximum sustainable spending
- acceptable debt or insurance risk
- minimum evidence before a lower-income transition
- conditions under which health, family, or burnout considerations dominate
  purely financial optimization
